# Player Guide

West Marches is different from a regular campaign. There's no DM who drives the story forward — that's your job as a player. Here's how to do it well.

---

## Your Character's Deal

Every character in Nerd World is a **mercenary** — an adventurer who takes on jobs for gold. You can play any class, race, or background you like, but the reason your character shows up to a session is always the job. This keeps things simple: the party doesn't need to be old friends or share a destiny. The coin is enough.

---

## Suggesting Sessions

**Players decide what happens in this world.** If you want to explore a forest, investigate a rumour, or return to that dungeon you didn't finish — say so. Post in the Messenger group:

> *"I'd love to run a session around the old ruins we spotted east of the river. Anyone interested? Could we get a DM?"*

That's all it takes. A DM will pick it up, prep something, and you'll have a session. The more specific your idea, the easier it is for a DM to build around it.

You don't need permission. You don't need a reason. Curiosity is enough.

---

## Revisiting Places

The world doesn't reset between sessions. If a party clears a dungeon, it stays cleared — but that doesn't mean there's nothing left to find. Locations change over time:

- Something new might have moved in
- An ally might have turned on the party
- A sealed door might now be open
- Rumours might point back to somewhere already visited

Going back somewhere is never a waste of time. The world is alive, and what you find the second time may be nothing like the first.

---

## Before the Session

- **Know your character.** Have your D&D Beyond sheet open and know what your abilities do. A 3–6 hour session moves fast.
- **Bring a goal.** Even a vague one. *"I want to find out what's in the north"* is better than waiting to be told what to do.
- **Coordinate in Messenger.** If a DM has announced a session, reply in the group. Let them know who's coming so they can pitch the difficulty right.

---

## At the Table

West Marches sessions are self-contained. The DM has prepped something specific — lean into it. Some tips:

- **Make decisions.** The DM has put things in the world for you to interact with. Engage with them. Prod them. Break them if you like.
- **Share the spotlight.** Sessions are 3–6 hours with a group. Make sure everyone gets moments, not just the loudest voices.
- **Take notes.** You don't have to, but someone at the table should. NPC names, locations, hooks you didn't follow — this stuff matters later.
- **Respect the clock.** Sessions end when they end. Don't disappear into an optional side room with 20 minutes left if the main thing isn't resolved.

---

## After the Session

- **Update your XP.** The DM will post the amount in Messenger and in the session report. Add it to your D&D Beyond sheet.
- **Share what you learned.** Post in Messenger. Other players want to know what's out there — a fragment of lore, an NPC name, a place worth returning to. The shared history builds one conversation at a time.
- **Level up if you hit the threshold.** Do it between sessions on D&D Beyond, not at the start of the next one.

---

## The One Rule

West Marches lives or dies on player initiative. If everyone waits for someone else to suggest a session, nothing happens. If everyone waits for a DM to tell them where to go, nothing interesting happens.

**Be the player who posts the idea. Be the DM who picks it up.**
